## First Impressions

I finally finished binge-watching Silicon Valley, and I didn’t expect to have this many thoughts about it.

I went into it thinking it would just be a funny sitcom about tech, but it ended up feeling very forward-looking. It’s actually a really interesting way, as an aspiring engineer, to see the business side of everything while still being entertained.

## What I Loved

What stood out to me the most is how the show balances humor with real insight into tech and startups. It doesn’t just make fun of the industry, it kind of explains the craziness behind every founder.

You get to see how ideas turn into companies, how messy that process is, and how much of it is uncertainty.

I also liked how it shows both sides of being technical:

- the excitement of building something new  
- the pressure of turning it into something that actually survives  

## What Felt Real

A lot of the emotional side of the show felt surprisingly real and hit pretty deeply.

Seeing Richard constantly go back and forth between confidence and panic felt very accurate. There are moments where he seems completely unsure of himself, and then other moments where he steps up and becomes a strong leader.

That whole cycle of:

- failure  
- small wins  
- more failure  
- trying again  

felt very true to what building things is actually like.

One thing I really liked is that the show doesn’t portray success as a straight path. It’s more about persistence than brilliance. Richard isn’t always the most confident or put-together, but he keeps going. That felt more realistic than the typical “genius founder” narrative.

## What Didn’t Feel Real

Some of the humor and situations are definitely exaggerated, and I don’t think that style of humor would really work if the show came out today.

But honestly, that’s part of what made it refreshing. It doesn’t feel like anything current.

## Favorite Characters

My favorite characters were Gilfoyle and Jared.

They’re both kind of unhinged in completely different ways, but they also clearly care a lot about the company.

- Gilfoyle is super dry and cynical, but always sharp  
- Jared is chaotic but somehow incredibly loyal  

That combination made them really fun to watch.

All the characters are well thought out, and even though they can be a little annoying at times, they’re all still very lovable.

## The AI Aspect (SPOILER)

One thing that really stood out to me was how ahead of its time the show felt, especially with the AI aspects toward the end.

Watching it now, it almost feels less like satire and more like a preview of where things were heading. A lot of what felt exaggerated at the time doesn’t feel that far off anymore. It reminded me a bit of Black Mirror in that sense.

## Ending Thoughts

The ending was a little sad, but also really satisfying. It leaves you with the feeling that even though things didn’t go perfectly, the journey still mattered.

Overall, I think the show does a really good job of portraying a visionary who goes through the ups and downs of failure and success and continues to get back up every time.

It also made me think about what it actually takes to build something, not just technically, but mentally.

Also… I was genuinely sad after finishing it. That’s how I know it was good.
