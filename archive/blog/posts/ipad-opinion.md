I’ve had my iPad for about 2 months now, and I’ve even traveled with it during that time.

I have the iPad Air 7 and the Apple Pencil Pro, and it’s ended up becoming a really interesting in-between device for me. Somewhere between my laptop and my phone.

## What I Actually Use It For

I don’t actually use my iPad that much for research. Most of my work is still on my laptop since I’m usually typing, coding, or managing a lot of tabs.

But I use it a lot for:
- watching YouTube  
- reading PDFs, especially on planes  
- journaling and planning  
- calling my boyfriend (long distance)  
- TA work and teaching  

It’s lightweight, easy to carry, and just feels more relaxed to use than a laptop.

## The Biggest Difference: YouTube vs Doomscrolling

One thing I notice is how differently I use my iPad compared to my phone.

On my phone, I tend to doomscroll. It’s fast, distracting, and hard to stop.

On my iPad, I’m way more intentional. I’ll open YouTube and actually watch something I chose, instead of endlessly scrolling.

The bigger screen makes it feel more like I’m choosing what to consume instead of just reacting.

I also like that my iPad has really good speakers, and when it’s propped up it basically becomes a lightweight TV. I can watch things while cooking, in bed, or even in my office at school.

## No Notifications, No Social Media

This is a big one.

I don’t have notifications or most social media on my iPad, and it completely changes how it feels to use. I only have Slack for academic work purposes.

It’s quieter. Less chaotic. I can focus a lot more.

It honestly feels like a “safe” device compared to my phone, which is full of distractions.

## TA Life: Why It’s Actually Really Useful

As a TA, my iPad has been super useful.

I can keep all my notes, explanations, and diagrams in one place. Students are also way more responsive when I’m writing on an iPad because I can scroll instead of constantly erasing a board.

I’ve done both, and writing on a board gets exhausting fast.

It’s also easier to prep material. I can project my iPad for students while still having my laptop open with notes, extra explanations, or even my own research.

When a student asks something, I can just draw it immediately without a lot of effort, which makes a big difference.

## Apple Pencil: Is It Worth It?

For me, yes.

Writing with the Apple Pencil feels smooth and natural once you get used to it. The biggest thing is finding the right pen style and size so your handwriting looks how you want it to.

I do notice I’m a little slower when I try to make my writing look nice, but overall I really enjoy it.

## Apps I Use

I try to avoid subscriptions when I can, so I prefer apps I can use long-term without paying monthly.

- CollaNote: probably my favorite notes app if you don’t want a subscription (one-time payment)  
- Zotero: great for reading PDFs and research papers, especially when traveling  
- Slack: easy to send files between devices  
- Pinterest: I love scrolling for visual inspiration, and it feels way better on a bigger screen  

## Journaling and Daily Use

I also use my iPad for journaling and manifesting.

I made my own templates using Canva and created daily pages I can fill out whenever I want. It makes it feel a lot more personal and fun to use.

## It’s Actually Lighter (and Mentally Easier)

One thing I noticed pretty quickly is how much lighter my backpack feels.

Instead of carrying notebooks, papers, and everything else, I just carry my iPad.

As someone who has anxiety about losing things, it’s nice knowing everything is in one place.

## The Downsides

It’s not that useful for heavy research.

If you need to:
- type a lot  
- code  
- manage a ton of tabs  

you’re still going to use your laptop.

Being stuck in the Apple Ecosystem: Since I have both an iPhone and an iPad, I can easily switch FaceTime calls between them or move things around seamlessly, which for me is a plus.

## So… Is It Worth It?

I think it depends on how you use it.

It’s worth it if you:
- like watching videos on the side  
- want a lightweight device  
- enjoy writing or journaling  
- want something less distracting than your phone  

It’s probably not worth it if you:
- mostly type  
- rely on complex workflows or lots of tabs  
- expect it to replace your laptop  

For me, it’s worth it because it feels like the perfect in-between device. My phone is distracting, and my laptop is heavy and work-focused. The iPad sits right in the middle.

## IMPORTANT: You Have to Customize It

This made the biggest difference for me.

You will use and enjoy your iPad so much more if you customize it.

Changing app icons, adding widgets, and organizing everything the way you like completely changes the experience.

I made mine pink and cute, and it honestly makes me want to use it more.

That alone made it feel worth it.
