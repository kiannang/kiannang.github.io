Welcome to my little corner of the internet. I'm Kianna, a PhD student at UC Merced working on computer vision, robotic perception, and human-centered AI.

This blog started as a place where I thought I would write mostly about research, projects, papers, and ideas. I still want it to be that sometimes. But the more time I spend in technical spaces, the more I realize that the work is never just technical. There are feelings inside it too.

There is uncertainty. There is confidence that comes and goes. There is pressure, comparison, excitement, disappointment, ambition, burnout, joy, and the quiet process of figuring out who you are while trying to become good at something difficult.

I want this blog to have room for all of that.

## Why I'm writing

I've realized that a lot of what I learn doesn't really stick unless I write it down. Sometimes that means writing about a paper, a concept, or something I'm building. Other times, it means writing through a feeling I don't fully understand yet.

I want a space where I can slow down and fully digest what it feels like to be in a technical career, especially as someone still learning how to advocate for herself, take up space, and trust her own instincts.

This blog is my way of keeping track of both the intellectual and emotional parts of that process.

It's also a way to think more clearly. Writing forces me to pause long enough to ask what I actually mean, what I actually feel, and what I want to carry forward.

## What to expect

No strict schedule, no content strategy. Just posts when I have something I need to understand more honestly.

That might include:

- Things I'm building or experimenting with
- Concepts I'm trying to understand
- Thoughts on papers or ideas
- Reflections on PhD life and technical spaces
- Feelings I need to process instead of ignore
- Lessons I'm learning about confidence, self-advocacy, and boundaries
- Thoughts on shows, games, or anything else I've been into lately

## A note on audience

I'm still writing for a general technically curious reader, not necessarily someone in my exact subfield. But I'm also writing for myself.

I want this to be a safe space to be reflective without needing everything to sound polished, certain, or perfectly professional. Some posts may be technical. Some may be personal. Some may sit somewhere in between, because honestly, that is where a lot of life happens.

## To start

Thanks for being here. More soon, with more honesty.

— Kianna
